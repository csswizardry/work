Frequently Asked Questions

What can you offer?
My skillset is highly specific; I deal with CSS architecture for big sites and apps, with a strong focus on front-end performance, scalability, team-based workflows, pragmatism and rationalisation, and agile and business-focused development. I can also offer training, workshops and talks based around the above, and I make a decent cup of tea.
Would you be interested in…?
Quite possibly! The biggest thing I want to get out of this adventure is variety, so get in touch and we’ll see!
You probably wouldn’t want to work for someone so small, would you?
Unfortunately I get this one quite a lot. Please don’t think that, because I speak at conferences and am used to working on larger products/projects et cetera, that I am a Prima Donna who doesn’t want to work with smaller clients. I want variety in my work now – if you’re nice, talented people who care about what you do, I want to work with you.
Are you looking for anything full time?
Probably not, but never say never! If you have a role which will allow a lot of flexibility – perhaps an evangelism type gig – so that I can continue speaking, work remotely, and which gives a lot of freedom, then drop me a mail.
Are you looking for anything long term?
Again, most likely not unless the right thing comes up. I really want flexibility, I don’t want to be tied down or feel locked into anything for too long. Short term contracts – and even joining your product for an initial few sprints – is the kind of thing I’m after, getting people started on their way to sustain themselves.
Do you have a day rate?
I do, but I only use it when necessary. I find that a lot of the work we do as developers cannot be watered down into time-based worth. I could spend three hours consulting with you and provide thousands of pounds’ worth of insight. Conversely, I could spend three days on your product and not deliver anywhere near as much value. I am keen, where possible, to work to a more value-based approach. We can discuss the boring money stuff later, let’s get to know each other first.
